About the Database

This database contains over 525,000 political fundraising emails collected from 2015 to 2025, with more than half originating from the high-activity years of 2022 and 2024. All emails were sent to a single Gmail account, providing a longitudinal view of political fundraising messages.

Peak Activity: More than 50% of messages were sent during 2022 and 2024, reflecting increased activity during election years.

Data Sources

The senders include a mix of political and advocacy organizations representing both major political parties:

  • Federal political committees
  • State and local political committees
  • Political advocacy organizations
  • Partisan news aggregators
  • Issue-based organizations
  • Some educational or commercial organizations with ideological ties

Data Characteristics

Each message includes comprehensive metadata and content analysis:

  • Full message content with HTML-to-text conversion
  • Sender information and domain extraction
  • Political party affiliation detection
  • Disclaimer presence identification
  • URL extraction and cataloging
  • Temporal metadata (date, time components)
Identifying Political Committees: The presence of disclaimers (phrases like "paid for by") is a reliable indicator distinguishing traditional political committees from advocacy organizations and other entities.

Technical Implementation

The database is built using Python and includes:

  • SQLite database with full-text search capabilities
  • Political party classification
  • Domain-based sender identification
  • Fundraising platform detection (ActBlue, WinRed, etc.)
  • Hosting generously provided by Datasette Cloud
View code on GitHub

Data Considerations

Some emails may appear multiple times due to list sharing among committees and organizations. Not every political campaign or committee is represented in this data.

The dataset offers researchers, journalists and analysts a window into political fundraising communication strategies and how messaging spreads among political actors.
